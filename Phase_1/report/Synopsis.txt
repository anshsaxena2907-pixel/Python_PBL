PROBLEM STATEMENT:
Credit card fraud is a major financial issue worldwide, causing significant losses to banks and customers. Detecting fraudulent transactions is challenging due to the highly imbalanced nature of the dataset, where fraudulent transactions are very rare compared to legitimate ones.

PROBLEM DESCRIPTION:
Credit card fraud is a serious issue in the financial sector, leading to huge monetary losses for banks and customers. With the increasing use of online transactions, detecting fraudulent activities has become more challenging. Fraudulent transactions are very rare compared to legitimate ones, making the dataset highly imbalanced. Traditional rule-based systems fail to detect new and evolving fraud patterns effectively.
This project aims to build a machine learning-based classification system that can automatically detect whether a transaction is legitimate or fraudulent. By analyzing transaction-level data such as amount, time, and other features, the system can help financial institutions take quick actions and reduce financial risks.
